When should you hire an RPO?

Leave a Comments / RPO / By Admin

Consider this: you used to need to hire about three or four people each year. In such a case, simply having a few good HR personnel to take care of your recruitment will do the trick. But in the COVID-19 and post-COVID world, who’s to say your hiring requirements will remain the same? Already the world has seen massive layoffs and an employment crisis this year due to COVID.

Even outside of such circumstances, recruitment processes have tended to get tough – this is why RPOs emerged. A firm may approach an RPO in the event that their in-house recruitment team is unable to scale to the volume of requirements. Companies that are looking at such a situation may fare better with a change in their talent acquisition procedure.

A Complicated Process

Recruitment itself is a generally complicated process. The industry standards are constantly evolving and difficult to understand for those who aren’t well versed with the nuances of this unique market. Besides, those who aren’t used to having frequent hiring requirements have a hard time working through recruitment. It can become all-consuming extremely quickly – which is where an RPO comes in.

Your requirements can be answered quickly with strategic plans crafted by experts. No matter how often your requirements arise, you can (be) rest assured that you’ll be covered when partnering with an RPO.

Seasonal Hiring

Often, hiring requirements go beyond traditional requirements. Companies that bear down during specific times of the year have a need for a set of employees for a specific period of time. This could be due to new product launches, events, moving locations (which, as a matter of fact, do happen less often, but are still a dire time).

In such a situation, the usual recruitment team and resources cannot scale to meet these additional needs. Often, they lack the expertise in the recruitment field to sift through the market and find the ideal candidates. This is where RPOs can jump in to fill the gap. RPOs can be hired on project bases as well, which can help the existing recruitment team with their analytical understanding of the industry.

COVID-19 and Beyond

As discussed above, COVID-19 has brought about an entirely unique atmosphere in the job market. The hire-and-fire basis that most companies are working on is anxiety-inducing for the candidates and has long-term disadvantages for companies as well.

RPOs are known for their short turnaround time and efficient work. With the environment that COVID-19 has brought into the industry, RPOs have adapted to continue to provide optimum solutions. The situation that is prevailing during the pandemic is a unique one. RPOs spend their time studying this to work out new and more efficient ways to make hiring a more efficient and fast process. This short-term assistance may help save your company valuable time and resources in the long term.
