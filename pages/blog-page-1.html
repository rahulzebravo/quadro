6 Best Business Development strategies for Staffing Agencies

Leave a Comments / RPO / By Admin

Staffing agencies frequently ask themselves, “how do we find more clients?” As a staffing company owner, you won’t need anyone to inform you that your business won’t survive long without a consistent supply of more clients. Outreach is often time-consuming; in a perfect world, clients will approach you for his or her recruitment needs. But with a globally interconnected marketplace and a mountain of competition, that is unlikely to happen in most cases.

Business development is a continuous process and is the most important factor when it comes to the growth of a company. No matter what time of the year it is, the challenge of attracting more clients always remains the same. A staffing agency has to quantify more clients in order to have enough probability to reach revenue goals.

We know there are a lot of ways out there and it is always difficult to choose out of them for your industry and your company. But below are the five proven business development strategies for staffing companies to consistently work on: –

Cold calling: – A lot of people believe that cold calling does not work, but it is still the best source for the first approach to sell/market your product in B2B and B2C industry. It gives you direct access to the decision-maker for your purpose. It keeps you updated on what your competitor is offering. 82% of buyers say they have accepted meetings with salespeople after a series of contacts beginning with cold calls. Yes, the fortune lies in follow-ups and prospecting them the right way.

Direct mail marketing: – This may sound old school in an era where emails and calls are quicker to reach. But if done right, direct mail marketing has also proved to work for every industry and business that has tried it. A postcard with discount and speaking about your services is old age, concentrate on WHY and the stats turn in your favor. There are enough things you can plan to send to your prospects to create an impression. Direct mail campaigns provide a high ROI and even a better ROI than paid ads. It is used to be memorable when it is directed directly to the decision-maker.

Email Marketing: – There will be times when your emails will be buried in other ten thousand emails of clients, they might end up in spam, you might not have the ratio you are looking for but when the best way to keep your prospects updated about what you are and what you do, email marketing comes as the best solution.

Sourcing Tools: – No, we are not talking about tools that help your recruiters to pull out the contact info on candidates, we are talking about job boards. Yes, a very common practice by recruiters but a very effective way to reach to the hiring managers attention. Visit any job posting websites and find the niche you can work for and then find the exact resume to go and pitch to the hiring manager. They are known as low hanging fruits. There are times when you see repeated posting from the same client, that is when you understand they are either constantly looking for a vendor or they haven’t found the right candidate yet.

Networking: –From a junior level employee to the C-suite executive, everyone benefits from networking. At the end of the day recruiting is people’s business and that said networking plays a vital role in promoting and marketing your company. There are a lot of networking meetups locally that helps business network. Connecting with local/state staffing associations also keeps you updated with the industry. One of the biggest networking groups that have helped business along the way is BNI.

Split-Fee Networks: – As we all know the gig economy is on a rise, hiring a freelancer to help you close your open position is a common practice these days. At the same way, there are split-fee platforms that help you work with clients who are looking to give out work on contingent bases. This not only starts creating some momentum for you but also help you network with people and give you an idea about the market and the demand that is out there to fulfil. There are a lot of split-fee networks out there, you need to see their reviews and study them before starting to work with them.

A BONUS fact, you can also explore Franchise staffing models, there are more 10 staffing franchise companies which you can partner with and start working. Owning a staffing franchise is often an incredibly lucrative investment.
